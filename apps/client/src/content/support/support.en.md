# How to use Oryzae

Small things you might wonder about while living with Oryzae, gathered in one place — including the small details we don't cover on the [landing page](https://oryzae.ephemere.io) or in the welcome tour.

## 1. Introduction — the four places

Oryzae is a writing app that turns around a single question. You set a question, write entries into it, jar them, wait for fermentation, then read a letter. Four places carry that rhythm.

### Editor

A quiet, vertical (or horizontal) page for daily writing. Optional typing effects bring a small body to each keystroke.

### Jar

Where your question lives, together with the entries linked to it. Both the place fermentation prepares in, and a way to look at the week from above.

### Board

A flat surface for laying out entries, snippets, and photos. Daily and weekly modes — memory takes shape through relations rather than time.

### Letter

Each weekend, a 500-character letter and 3–5 keywords arrive — distilled by koji, lactic, and yeast. Not a summary; a small ember to keep you writing.

## 2. The basic rhythm

A typical week with Oryzae looks roughly like this.

### 1. Set a question

Create one from the header or the Jar. It doesn't have to be polished — "Why…", "How might I…", a sentence whose answer you don't yet have, is plenty. You can edit it any time.

### 2. Write entries

Write in the editor whenever you like. Titles are optional. Entries auto-save every few seconds; the status bar shows editing / saving / saved.

### 3. Tie an entry to your question (pickle it)

On save, choose "pickle" to link the entry to your current question. Entries that aren't tied to a question stay out of the Jar — and out of fermentation.

### 4. Wait for fermentation

On the weekend, the week's entries that are tied to your question pass through three stages — koji decomposes, lactic writes a letter, yeast offers keywords. Replies are deliberately delayed; that is the rhythm Oryzae aims for.

### 5. Read the letter and keywords

When fermentation completes, you can open a 500-character letter and 3–5 keywords from the Jar. Not a summary — a comment written deliberately a little off from your own vocabulary, to keep your thinking moving.

### 6. Look back from the Board

On the Board, the week's entries, snippets, and photos sit side by side. Daily and weekly views let you move between a single day's density and the whole week's flow.

## 3. Editor essentials

The editor is the heart of Oryzae. A few small features take care of the act of writing itself.

### Vertical / horizontal

Switch between vertical and horizontal writing from the toolbar at any time. Default is vertical, tuned for Japanese and Chinese.

### Mincho / Gothic

Toggle between mincho (serif, Noto Serif JP) and gothic (sans, Noto Sans JP) when you want to change the breath of the page.

### Auto-save

Saves automatically every few seconds. The status — editing, saving, saved — sits in the status bar.

### Snippets

Select a sentence in the body and a "snippet" button appears. Lift up to 50 characters out as a snippet, to lay them next to others on the Board later.

### Linking to a question

On save, you can link the entry to your current question. Only linked entries enter the Jar and become candidates for fermentation.

### Writing stats

Open the stats panel from the toolbar for character / line / paragraph counts and an estimated reading time.

### Voice input

Toggle voice input from the microphone icon in the toolbar. Chrome is recommended. Microphone permission is required.

### Fullscreen

When you want everything else out of view. Click again to return to the normal layout.

## 4. Editor effects

Small effects that bring a touch of body to the act of writing. Each one can be toggled individually from the settings icon at the top right of the editor.

| Effect | What happens | Good for |
|---|---|---|
| Focus mode | Dims everything except the paragraph the cursor is in. | Sinking into a single paragraph at a time. |
| Time Inscription | Encodes per-key dwell time and speed into character size, weight, and pressure bleed. | Leaving rhythm and emphasis on the page itself. |
| Eraser Trace | Deleted characters fade out gradually instead of disappearing instantly. | Looking back at the path of revision. |
| AMP / ASMR | Picks up keystroke sounds via the mic and re-amplifies them through filters and a compressor. | Days when you simply love the sound of writing. Mic permission required. |
| Voice Dynamics | Reflects the volume of your voice, picked up by the mic, into character weight and size. | Speaking aloud while you write or think. Mic permission required. |
| Ghost | Characters leave a soft afterimage that blurs and fades. Two modes: block (on commit) and dust (per key). | Watching the rhythm of writing itself. |

Size, scatter, blur, and duration can all be tuned from the settings panel.

## 5. Account & settings

From the Account page, you can change the following.

### Nickname

The name shown inside the app. It doesn't have to be your real name.

### Email

A confirmation email is sent to the new address; clicking the link there finishes the change. Accounts using Google sign-in only can't change the email here.

### Password

Both the current and a new password (6+ characters) are required. Not shown for accounts that use only Google sign-in.

### Theme

Switch between light and dark.

### Language

Switch between Japanese and English. The setting follows the account across devices.

### Writing stats

From the Account page, see your total entries and total characters written so far.

## 6. Frequent questions

### Where is what I write stored?

It is sent to Oryzae's server over SSL and stored in an encrypted database (Supabase). No-one but your authenticated account can read it. See the [Privacy Policy](/privacy) for details.

### What does the AI read?

Only the question you set, and the entries you have tied to that question. Drafts and casual notes that aren't tied to a question stay out of fermentation.

### When does fermentation happen?

Roughly on the weekend, against the entries linked to your question that week. Fermentation may be skipped depending on entry volume and a small time gate.

### How do I delete an entry?

Open the entry from the list and choose delete from the menu. Even if it was linked to a question, deleting it removes it from fermentation as well.

### How many questions can I have?

You can keep several questions in the Jar at once. We recommend choosing one as the "current question" and tying entries to that, so the fermentation has a clear focus.

### Do I have to write vertically?

No. Vertical is the default, but you can switch to horizontal from the toolbar at any time. The font flips between mincho (serif) and gothic (sans) too.

### Can I write in languages other than Japanese?

Yes — multilingual input is supported, including English. Fermentation responds in the language you write in. Vertical writing is tuned for Japanese and Chinese.

### Voice input or mic-based effects don't work

Voice input, AMP / ASMR, and Voice Dynamics all need microphone access. We recommend Chrome. Please check your OS / browser microphone permissions, including the per-site permission for this app.

### Can I use Oryzae on my phone?

You can read and jot down short notes, but Oryzae is designed for sitting down and writing on a desktop. To get the most out of vertical writing, the effects, and the stats, desktop Chrome is recommended.

### Is there a paid plan?

Oryzae is currently a research preview. Pricing has not been set, and use is free. Any future changes will be announced on the landing page and by email.

### How is Oryzae different from Pickles?

Pickles was a place to write the journal itself. Oryzae sets a question at the centre and adds the time around the writing — a fermentation process that returns your own words to you in a different shape. Data migration from Pickles is not currently offered.

### How do I delete my account?

For now, account deletion is handled manually. Please get in touch via the contact below. The same goes for exporting data you've written so far.
