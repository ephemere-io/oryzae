# Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 9, 2026

Oryzae ("the Service") is a journaling app provided by Ferment Media Research ("we", "us"). This policy explains, in plain language, what information we handle when you use the Service.

## 1. What this policy says, in short

- We store the entries you write, your email address, your nickname, and similar account-related information.
- We use that information only to operate the Service and to run the AI "fermentation" feature.
- We do not sell your data to third parties for advertising.
- You can ask us to delete your data or send you a copy of it. Contact details are at the bottom of this page.

## 2. Who we are

- Operator: Ferment Media Research
- Contact: oryzae@ephemere.io
- Service URL: https://oryzae.ephemere.io

## 3. Information we collect

When you use the Service, the following information is stored on our systems.

### 3.1 Account information

- Email address (for sign-in and notifications)
- A hash of your password (only when you use password authentication)
- Google account information (display name, email, avatar URL — only when you sign in with Google)
- Nickname and avatar URL that you set yourself
- Internal user ID

### 3.2 What you write

- Entry contents (the body of your journal — up to 100,000 characters per entry)
- Entry edit history (snapshots)
- Questions (the prompts or themes you set)
- The links you make between questions and entries
- Snippets (short fragments you cut out from your entries)
- Card layout on the Board (position, rotation, size, etc.)
- Photos and captions you add to the Board

### 3.3 Output of the AI fermentation

- Keywords extracted by the AI
- Letters generated by the AI
- The schedule state of fermentation (e.g. last fermentation timestamp)

### 3.4 Information collected automatically

- Usage events (page views, feature usage)
- Error logs and stack traces (for debugging)
- Technical information about your browser, OS, and device
- Your language preference (stored in a cookie called `NEXT_LOCALE`)

## 4. How we use information

We use the information we hold for the following purposes only.

1. Authenticating you (sign-in, sign-out, identity verification)
2. Storing, displaying, and searching what you have written
3. Running the fermentation feature — sending entries that you have tied to a question, plus the question itself, to an AI model so it can produce keywords and a letter for you
4. Sending operational emails (email-change confirmation, password reset, etc.)
5. Investigating bugs and keeping the Service running reliably
6. Analysing usage in aggregated, anonymised form to improve the product

## 5. About the AI fermentation feature

Fermentation, the heart of the Service, sends text to an external AI model (Claude, by Anthropic) for processing. We apply the following rules to that processing.

- The AI receives your entries and the questions.
- We use a commercial API so that the data we send is not used to train the AI provider's models.
- The output (keywords, letter) is stored in our database under your account.

The AI's output is for reference only — please do not rely on it as authoritative.

## 6. Third parties (sub-processors)

To run the Service, we rely on the providers below. Each one handles your information under its own privacy policy.

| Provider | Purpose | Data shared |
|---|---|---|
| Supabase (Supabase Inc.) | Database, authentication, file storage | Account information, entries, images — all stored data |
| Anthropic (Anthropic PBC) | AI model for fermentation | The question and the entries tied to it, only |
| Vercel (Vercel Inc.) | Application hosting | Network traffic (encrypted in transit) |
| Resend (Resend, Inc.) | Transactional email (confirmation, password reset) | Email address, email body |
| Sentry (Functional Software, Inc.) | Error tracking | Error logs, stack traces, user ID |
| PostHog (PostHog Inc.) | Product analytics | Usage events, user ID, email address |
| Upstash (Upstash, Inc.) | Cache and rate limiting (optional) | Temporary session and cache data |

We do not sell or share your data with third parties beyond what is described here.

If we receive a lawful disclosure request, we may disclose the minimum necessary data to comply.

## 7. Where and how long we keep data

- Data is stored in data centres operated by Supabase, primarily in regions in the United States. This may include transfer outside your country of residence.
- We retain your data for as long as your account is active.
- When you ask us to delete your account, we will delete your account, entries, and related data within a reasonable period. We may retain limited data where the law requires us to.

## 8. Your rights

You have the right to ask us to:

- Disclose the data we hold about you
- Correct inaccurate information
- Delete your account and your data
- Stop using your data for a particular purpose
- Stop providing your personal data to sub-processors

To make a request, please email the address in the contact section. We will respond within a reasonable period after verifying your identity.

You can update your nickname, email, and password yourself from the account settings page (`/account`).

## 9. Cookies

We use cookies only as needed to operate the Service.

- `NEXT_LOCALE`: remembers the display language you chose
- Authentication tokens: keep you signed in
- PostHog analytics cookies: aggregated, anonymised usage analysis

If you disable cookies in your browser, some features (such as staying signed in) may stop working.

## 10. Children

The Service is not intended for children under 18. Please do not use the Service if you are under 18.

## 11. Security

All traffic is encrypted with TLS (HTTPS). Passwords are stored as hashes — we never keep them in plain text. No internet service can promise absolute security, but we apply reasonable, ongoing measures to protect your data.

## 12. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy when the Service or applicable law changes. For material changes, we will notify you in the Service or by email. The latest version always lives on this page.
